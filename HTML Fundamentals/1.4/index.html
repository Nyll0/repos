meatballs in dill sauce

Shoping list
  • Carrots x3
  • Potatos 0,5kg
  • Minced pork 0,5kg
  • Chicken stock
  • Dill
Recepi
  1. Whisk an egg into the bowl with the ground meat and soaked bread crumbs and add the spices: a flat teaspoon each of salt and herbes de Provence and half a teaspoon of pepper.
  2. Pour 1000 ml of broth into a medium, but wide pot. It can be chicken broth alone or even vegetable broth. Peel the carrots and cut them into shadowy slices and then add them to the broth. Bring the broth to a boil. Reduce the power to just enough to make it flicker slightly and boil it for about 10 minutes.
  3. Form walnut-sized balls from the meat mixture. Each pullet weighs about 50 grams. Transfer the meatballs carefully to the pot with the blinking broth. Stack them one next to the other, but do not move them from their place, as they are too tender at the beginning of cooking. I came out with 14 pullets evenly.
  4. Increase the power of the burner a little, so that the broth with the pullets begins to boil slightly again. Cook the meatballs in the broth without a lid for 15-20 minutes (low burner power).
  5. Pour about 1/3 cup of 30% creamer into a small bowl. Add to it a full tablespoon of wheat flour, such as cake flour or all-purpose flour. Mix the whole thing very thoroughly so that there are no lumps of flour in the cream. If the cream is at room temperature, it will be much easier for you to combine it with the flour than if the cream is taken straight out of the refrigerator.
  6. Powstanie bardzo gęsta masa, więc zanim dodasz ją do garnka, polecam najpierw kilka łyżek bulionu przelać do miseczki z mieszanką śmietankowo mączną. Zamieszaj całość i dopiero wlej do garnka. Bardzo ostrożnie zamieszaj zawartość garnka a następnie dodaj też pół pęczka siekanego koperku, czyli około 35 gramów zieleniny.
